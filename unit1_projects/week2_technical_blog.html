Week 2 - Margin, Border, and Padding

What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

Illustrated above is an example of the "Box Model." Understanding the fundamentals involved in manipulating the box model becomes priority when your goal is to properly size html elements on a webpage.

There are many specific commands that come into play when positioning elements (blocks of content) within your page. Today we will instead focus on providing a surface level understanding of what is involved in basic position of an element.

As you can see from the image above, the "Element Content" is encapsilated by several frames; "padding," "border," and "margin". These become important when determining the layout of your design. Below I will give a brief rundown of each section of the image. Its important to note that each of the "frames" can be adjusted by leveraging special css commands.

Element Content: The very thing you're attempting to position. This could be an image, text, or even a table.

Padding: Padding offers a small buffer between your border and your content.

Border: The border can be employed to create a visual mark between elements.

Margin: This will determine how close to rest next to other elements on the page.
